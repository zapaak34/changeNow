Frequently Asked Questions

Cryptocurrency is a digital currency secured by cryptography and operating on decentralized networks called blockchains. It enables peer-to-peer transactions without the need for intermediaries like banks.

A blockchain is a distributed ledger that records transactions across a network of computers. It's secure, transparent, and immutable, meaning once data is recorded, it cannot be altered retroactively.

A cryptocurrency wallet is a digital tool for storing, sending, and receiving cryptocurrencies. It consists of a public key (your wallet address) and a private key (your password to access funds).

Choose a reliable exchange like ChangeNow, create an account, complete verification, deposit funds, and buy cryptocurrencies. Start with small amounts and diversify your investments.

Use strong passwords, enable two-factor authentication, store your crypto in reputable wallets (hardware wallets for large amounts), never share your private keys, and be cautious of phishing attempts.

Popular cryptocurrencies include Bitcoin (BTC), Ethereum (ETH), Binance Coin (BNB), Cardano (ADA), Solana (SOL), Ripple (XRP), and Polkadot (DOT). Each serves different purposes in the crypto ecosystem.

Cryptocurrency legality varies by country. In most developed countries, cryptocurrencies are legal but regulated. It's important to check your local regulations and tax requirements for cryptocurrency transactions.
